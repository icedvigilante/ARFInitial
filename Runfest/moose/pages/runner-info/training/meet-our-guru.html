Meet Our Guru:  Deb Vitanza

Anchorage RunFest wants to help our runners in whatever way we can. We offer this training section merely as a guide. The advice you find here reflects the experience of our "running guru." We offer it with the best intentions but cannot guarantee nor be responsible for your outcomes.



ARF is excited to have Deb Vitanza working with us gathering helpful information on training for our participants. We have known Deb for a long time. If you check our Race History page you’ll see that Deb (under her former name Debbie Cropper) was our marathon champion in 2008 and 2009. But there’s a whole lot more to know about Deb. Read on.

Deb was born and raised in a small town in Norwich, NY where, as she says, “everybody knows your name.” She lived in Ventura, CA for almost twenty-four years and has lived in Alaska since 2002. Deb has a personal history of perseverance and dedication through trials in life and running. In fact, running became a lifeline for her in her mid thirties. Those of you just starting a running program take heart: Deb ran her first 10K at age 34 and surprised herself by placing in her age group. Someone said to her, “You’ve got a talent for running!” Thus running became her medicine, therapy, and a source of goals.

Deb has drawn many surprising rewards and blessings from her marathon travels. Most importantly, she notes, are the caring people, friendships, outpouring of community, and the camaraderie of people pulling together while pursuing their individual causes. She has shared her personal story with others and been privileged to hear theirs in return. A nice benefit to her experiences has been that her travels to all fifty states enabled her to collect teaching material to bring to her students back in Anchorage. She says she was “blessed beyond measure” to place in 41 states in overall, masters, or age group categories.

As of February 2015, Deb had run one hundred marathons and was the twenty-second person (and the third female) to complete that feat with all fifty state marathon finish times under four hours. Perhaps even more impressive is the fact that ninety-five of her marathons were completed under four hours and three of the other five were ultra trail runs. Her fastest marathon, a 3:09, didn’t happen until Deb was forty-nine -- when she “really gave up stubborn ways and followed a schedule.”

Recently, Deb has been a pacer working with MarathonPacing.com and has met amazing people. Pacing enables her to help others finish well, share, and care. It allows Deb to give back all she has learned to the best of her ability and to fulfill her passion of helping people believe and achieve. Providing helpful content for our BWLR training pages is another way Deb is giving back to the running community.

Deb says: "I hope as runners connect to the Big Wild Life Runs outreach in our training pages, that they will be empowered to be all they want to be and more! It’s about life -- truly our best marathon! Whatever your distance, give it your heart and truly enjoy the journey! I have run ALL distances from the mile to a 50K and succeeded in all distances. It is the mental training that is most important."
